Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about controls, devices, reactions, and safety. Filter by keyword search or by category.

🔍
QWhat is this site?General▾
It is an educational chemistry simulator where you combine elements and reagents and observe reactions and changes in pH, temperature, gases, precipitates, and more. You can safely experience and learn how reactions behave without handling real chemicals.
QIs it free? Do I need to register?General▾
Yes. No registration or installation is required — it runs for free in your browser.
QCan I switch between Japanese and English?General▾
Yes. Use the language switch on the top page, or the language option in the lab header, to switch between the Japanese and English versions.
QWho is it for?General▾
It suits learners from secondary school up to introductory university-level chemistry, and anyone curious about chemistry. It also works well for lesson preparation, review, and self-study.
QHow do I add reagents (elements / chemicals)?Usage▾
Tap a tile in the left library, or drag and drop it into the beaker. Both methods add reagents the same way.
QHow do I start a reaction?Usage▾
After adding reagents, press "Start ▶ React" at the bottom center. Some reactions require heating, and some require ventilation.
QWhat is the difference between Heat, Stir, and Ventilate?Usage▾
Heat warms the solution to drive reactions that need heating. Stir mixes to promote reaction and dissolution. Ventilate runs the fume hood to vent evolved gases and lower their concentration. Ventilation is essential for reactions that release hazardous gas.
QWhat are missions?Usage▾
A task shown in the center of the header. Follow the instructions to complete it. You can switch between Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced difficulty, and use "Show Hint" to reveal the reagent combination to use.
QCan I save my experiment results?Usage▾
Use the CSV button in the observation panel to export measured data. The PDF button generates a report summarizing the reagents used, equations, products, and observations, which you can print or save as PDF.
QWhat is the Reaction Index?Usage▾
A list of observable reactions. You can search chemical equations, ionic equations, hazard levels, and observations by keyword or tag. Open it from the lab header.
QHow do I clear the experiment history?Usage▾
Use the "Clear" button at the top right of the "Experiment History" section in the observation panel.
QWhich devices are supported?Devices▾
A desktop or laptop computer is recommended. Tablets can run the lab in landscape orientation. On smartphones and portrait-orientation tablets the full lab does not launch, but you can watch the reaction demonstrations.
QThe full lab does not start on my smartphone.Devices▾
By design, the full lab does not launch on smartphones; a computer is recommended. On a smartphone you can still watch the reaction demonstrations from the guidance screen.
QMy tablet shows a guidance screen instead of the lab.Devices▾
Rotate the tablet to landscape. The lab launches automatically in landscape. In portrait orientation only the demonstrations are available.
QIs there a recommended browser?Devices▾
The latest versions of Chrome, Edge, Safari, or Firefox are recommended. Older browsers may not display or run correctly.
QThere is sound / I want to turn it off.Devices▾
Use the "♪ SOUND" button in the lab header to toggle sound on and off.
QAre the reactions exactly like real ones?Reactions▾
No. Reactions, colors, flames, smoke, gases, temperature, pH, and so on include simplified representations for learning and do not perfectly reproduce real results. Actual reactions differ with concentration, purity, quantity, temperature, equipment, and more.
QWhat do the hazard stars (Lv.1–5) mean?Reactions▾
They indicate the hazard of a reaction or reagent: Lv.1 Safe / Lv.2 Caution / Lv.3 Dangerous (ventilation advised) / Lv.4 Very dangerous (ventilation required) / Lv.5 Lab-only / forbidden (lethal or dramatic).
QSome combinations do not react.Reactions▾
Some combinations do not react because of the ionization series or passivation (e.g. copper with dilute sulfuric acid, gold with dilute nitric acid, aluminium in concentrated nitric acid). See "Non-reacting combinations" in the Reaction Index for the reasons.
QWhat happens if an experiment "fails"?Reactions▾
If a hazardous gas reaches a lethal concentration, a warning and experiment-failed screen appears. It shows the cause and the correct procedure, so you can learn and try again.
QHow many reactions are included?Reactions▾
It includes about 47 reactions and 4 pH indicators, covering metals and acids, gas evolution, precipitation, metal displacement, catalysis, redox, passivation, and more.
QCan I perform these experiments for real as shown?Safety▾
No. This simulator is not a laboratory procedure. To perform real chemical experiments, always work in a properly equipped environment under the supervision of a qualified or knowledgeable instructor, following safety data sheets and your institution's standards.
QWhere can I read the safety notice and disclaimer?Safety▾
The full text is on the top page under "Safety Notice & Disclaimer." Please read it before use.
QThe screen is black or nothing happens.Troubleshooting▾
First, reload the page. If that does not help, update your browser to the latest version, try a different browser, or open it on a computer.
QThe "Enter" button is disabled.Troubleshooting▾
Tick the checkbox in the safety notice and disclaimer on the top page to enable the start button.
