VIRTUAL CHEMISTRY LAB / HOW TO USE

Getting Started

This is a quantitative simulator: drag or tap reagents into the beaker and use the control buttons to run reactions. This page explains the basic controls, how to read the screen, and device-specific use.

01 Three quick steps 02 Reading the screen 03 Adding reagents 04 Control buttons 05 Observation & history 06 Missions 07 Hazard levels 08 Index & report 09 Device-specific use

01 Three quick steps

  1. Add reagents — tap an element or reagent tile in the left library, or drag it into the beaker.
  2. Run the reaction — press "Start ▶ React" at the bottom center. Use Heat or Ventilate as needed.
  3. Observe — watch pH, temperature, gas concentration, and more change in the right panel. Reactions are logged in the Experiment History.

02 Reading the screen

Left: Reagent library

A search box plus Elements / Reagents / Indicators tabs. Tap or drag a tile into the beaker.

Center: Beaker

The reaction is drawn in real time. The chemical equation is shown above and the control buttons below.

Right: Observation panel

Meters and a trend graph for temperature, pH, pressure, conductivity, volume, and gas — plus reaction analysis and experiment history.

Top: Header

Mission, difficulty switch, hint, reaction index, how-to, Q&A, and sound controls.

03 Adding reagents

There are two ways — both add reagents in the same way.

👆 Tap to add (phones & tablets)

Just tap a tile in the library to drop it into the beaker. Comfortable even where finger-dragging is hard.

🖱 Drag to add (desktop)

Grab a tile and drag and drop it into the beaker.

Search to find things fast

The search box at the top left matches element names, reagent names, or chemical formulas. For example:

AgSilverAgNO₃ → any of these find silver / silver nitrate (AgNO₃).

04 Control buttons

ButtonFunction
HeatHeats the solution. Used for reactions that require heating (e.g. copper + concentrated sulfuric acid, burning sulfur).
StirStirs to promote reaction and dissolution.
VentilateRuns the fume hood, venting gases and lowering their concentration. Essential for reactions that release hazardous gas.
Start ▶ ReactStarts and stops the reaction.
STOPEmergency stop: turns off heating and turns on ventilation.
Reset ⟳Empties the beaker and returns to the start state. After completing a mission, advances to the next task.

05 Observation panel & history

The right panel updates its readings live during a reaction.

📊 Meters & graph

Temperature, pH, pressure, conductivity, volume, and gas concentration. pH and temperature trends are shown as a line graph.

🧪 Odor & gas

Shows the name, odor, hazard level, and concentration of any gas evolved.

🔬 Reaction analysis

Explains the chemical equation, ionic equation, reaction type, and hazard (with details in Learning Mode).

🕘 Experiment history

Logs reactions in order, e.g. Zn + 2HCl → H₂ evolved. Use "Clear" to erase.

06 Missions

Follow the mission in the center of the header to complete each task.

Difficulty

Switch between Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced. Missions are generated from the reaction database and sorted by hazard and heating requirements.

💡 Show Hint

Stuck? Press "Show Hint" to reveal the combination of reagents to use.

07 Hazard levels

Hazardous reagents and reactions show a hazard level (Lv.1–5) as stars. Look for the ⚠ mark on tiles, or in the reaction analysis and index.

★☆☆☆☆Lv.1 Safe — no issues under normal handling.
★★☆☆☆Lv.2 Caution — mild care needed, e.g. gas evolution.
★★★☆☆Lv.3 Dangerous — toxic gas or heat. Ventilation advised.
★★★★☆Lv.4 Very dangerous — large amounts of toxic gas. Ventilation required.
★★★★★Lv.5 Lab-only / forbidden — lethal or dramatic reactions.

08 Reaction index & report

⊕ Reaction Index

A reaction list you can open from inside the lab. Search chemical equations, ionic equations, hazard levels, and observations by keyword or tag.

📄 Report (PDF) / CSV

After an experiment, generate a summary of the reagents used, equations, products, and observations. Print or save as PDF, or export the measured data as CSV.

09 Device-specific use

🖥
Desktop / laptop (recommended)

Three-column layout. Add reagents by drag-and-drop or by tapping. All features are available.

📲
Tablet (landscape)

Rotating to landscape launches the laboratory. The beaker and observation panel scroll vertically, and the control buttons stay fixed at the bottom.

📲
Tablet (portrait)

The full simulator does not launch; a guidance screen prompts you to rotate. From there you can watch the reaction demonstrations.

📱
Smartphone

The full simulator does not launch. A guidance screen recommends using a computer, and you can watch the reaction demonstrations there.
